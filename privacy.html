Privacy Policy

Effective March 1, 2026 · Version 4.2

The short version: we built PrivacyPal so sensitive data never leaves your device. This policy explains the narrow slice of metadata we do process — and exactly what we don't.

On this page

1. What data we process 2. Your rights 3. Subprocessors 4. DPA & data transfers 5. Security 6. Contact

1. What data we process

PrivacyPal's entire product thesis is that your sensitive data — PII, PHI, account numbers, privileged communications — is detected and swapped on your device or inside your VPC, and never reaches our servers. We are not in the data-processing path for your AI prompts.

What we do collect

  • Account data: email, billing info, plan tier, team membership.
  • Telemetry: anonymous counters (policies triggered per hour, detector health). No prompt content, ever.
  • Audit metadata: if you opt into the hosted audit log, hashes of detector decisions — never the underlying text.

2. Your rights

Under GDPR, CCPA, and equivalent frameworks you have the right to access, correct, port, and delete your personal data. Write to privacy@privacypal.ai with your account email; we respond within 14 days.

3. Subprocessors

We use a short list of processors for account management and infrastructure. LLM providers are never on this list — because your data never reaches them through us.

  • Stripe (billing)
  • AWS (infrastructure — us-east-1, eu-west-1)
  • Postmark (transactional email)
  • Linear & Intercom (support)

4. DPA & data transfers

Standard Contractual Clauses for EEA/UK transfers are available on request. Enterprise customers can execute our DPA — it's pre-approved by most banking, healthcare, and legal procurement teams.

5. Security

SOC 2 Type II audited annually. Penetration tested twice a year by an external firm. Bug bounty at hackerone.com/privacypal. On-device detection means there is no central data store to breach in the first place — that's by design.
